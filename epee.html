What Is Épée?

Épée is one of the three Olympic fencing weapons and is unique in its simplicity and tactical depth. It is a thrust-only weapon, meaning touches are made using the tip. The entire body — from head to toe — is a valid target, making épée the most realistic descendant of historical dueling.

Because there is no right-of-way rule, épée rewards timing and counterattacks. Careful distance control, patience, and precision define a successful épée fencer.

Rules of Épée

  • No Right-of-Way: Both fencers can score simultaneously. Double touches occur if hits land within about 40 milliseconds.
  • Whole Body Target: Any part of the opponent — arms, legs, torso, even mask — is valid.
  • Scoring: A point is awarded when the tip of the épée registers a valid hit with sufficient pressure.
  • Bout Format: Standard bouts are three 3-minute periods or until one fencer reaches 15 touches.
  • Equipment: Standard protective gear is required: mask, jacket, plastron, glove, pants, socks, and an electric épée.
  • Penalties: Cards may be given for stepping off-strip, covering target area, or other rule violations.

Where to Learn Épée in Seattle

Seattle has several excellent fencing clubs offering épée classes for beginners to advanced athletes:
